Why Queens Buyers Move to Long Island

Hundreds of Queens families have made the move to Nassau County in the past decade. Here's what draws them — and what they need to know before they go.

More Space — Inside and Out

Nassau County offers detached single-family homes with driveways, garages, and real backyards. For families squeezed into a two-bedroom co-op or semi-detached in Queens, this is a major quality-of-life upgrade. More space for the same or similar price in many cases.

School Districts — Independently Rated

Nassau County has dozens of independent school districts, many of which are consistently rated among the best in New York State. Unlike NYC, where school zoning can be complex, Nassau County's district lines are usually clear and tied directly to property address.

Parking — Finally, a Garage

One of the most common complaints from Queens residents is parking. Nassau County homes almost universally include driveways, garages, and street parking with no alternate-side rules. For families with multiple cars, this alone can be transformative.

Lower Population Density

Nassau County's residential neighborhoods are quieter, less congested, and have a suburban pace that many families prefer as they grow. Same access to the city via LIRR or highway, without the density of Queens living.

The Nassau County Advantage

Nassau is the closest Long Island county to NYC — many neighborhoods border Queens directly. Valley Stream, Elmont, Floral Park, and New Hyde Park are mere minutes from the Queens border, meaning you don't sacrifice proximity for space.

Community Roots Are Here Too

The South Asian and Caribbean communities that built Queens have been expanding into Nassau County for two decades. Elmont, Valley Stream, Hempstead, and Cambria Heights (Queens border) have strong Guyanese, Indian, and Punjabi community presence.

Nassau County Property Tax — What You Need to Know

Nassau County property taxes are significant. Every Queens buyer moving to Long Island needs to factor them in. Here's what to know.

Effective Tax Rates Run 2.5%–3.5%

On a $600,000 Nassau County home, annual property taxes typically range from $12,000 to $18,000+, depending on the village and school district. Always request the current tax bill when evaluating any property.

School District Drives the Tax Bill

The school district is often the largest portion of your Nassau County tax bill. Highly-rated districts (Rockville Centre, Garden City, Malverne) come with higher tax bills. Less well-known districts may mean lower taxes — a real trade-off to evaluate.

STAR Exemption — Apply After Closing

The Basic STAR (School Tax Relief) exemption can reduce your school taxes by roughly $1,000–$1,500/year if this is your primary residence. Enhanced STAR is available for homeowners 65+ meeting income requirements. Apply through Nassau County assessor's office after closing.

Tax Grievance Process — Many Homeowners Win

Nassau County has a formal Small Claims Assessment Review (SCAR) process. Many homeowners successfully challenge their assessments, especially shortly after purchase if the sale price supports a lower assessed value. Third-party services handle this on a contingency basis.

Frequently Asked Questions — Long Island / Nassau County

Honest answers to common questions about buying in Nassau County, Long Island.

Is Long Island better than Queens to raise a family?
Many Queens families move to Nassau County for more space, larger yards, and better-rated school districts. Long Island offers more single-family detached homes with driveways and backyards. However, Nassau County property taxes are significantly higher than NYC. Whether it's "better" depends on your priorities — space and schools versus Queens affordability and transit. Nitin helps families compare both markets honestly and make the right choice for their situation.
What are Nassau County property taxes like?
Nassau County has some of the highest property taxes in New York State. Effective tax rates run approximately 2.5%–3.5% of assessed value, varying by village and school district. For a $600,000 home, annual taxes can range from $12,000 to $18,000+. The STAR exemption can reduce school taxes for primary residents. Nassau County also has a formal tax grievance (SCAR) process that many homeowners use successfully to reduce their assessed value. Nitin can walk you through the tax picture for any specific property you're considering.

Is Hempstead a good place to buy?
The Town of Hempstead is the largest municipality on Long Island and contains dozens of villages and hamlets with very different characteristics. Rockville Centre, Garden City, and Lynbrook are among Long Island's most desirable. Elmont, Valley Stream, and West Hempstead offer solid value for buyers from Queens. Hempstead village itself is more affordable but has different school district ratings.
