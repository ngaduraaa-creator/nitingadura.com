About Springfield Gardens, Queens

Springfield Gardens (ZIP 11413) is an affordable, community-oriented neighborhood in Southeast Queens that has been quietly building strength as a place to live and invest. Situated directly adjacent to JFK Airport and bordered by Idlewild Park — one of Queens' most underappreciated natural spaces with trails, wetlands, and green space — Springfield Gardens offers a suburban character within city limits at some of the most accessible price points in Southeast Queens.

The community is predominantly Caribbean and South Asian, with large Guyanese, Trinidadian, Jamaican, Haitian, and growing South Asian populations. The neighborhood's family-oriented character is evident in its quiet residential streets, well-maintained semi-detached homes, and active community organizations. PS 132 and PS 137 serve the neighborhood's elementary students. Springfield Gardens has been an improving area in recent years — with infrastructure investment, new businesses, and rising interest from first-time buyers who have discovered its value proposition relative to pricier adjacent neighborhoods.

For first-time homebuyers seeking an affordable Queens entry point with strong community ties and JFK employment access, Springfield Gardens stands out. The neighborhood regularly offers single-family homes in a price range accessible to buyers using FHA financing or down payment assistance programs — making homeownership achievable for families who might otherwise be priced out of Southeast Queens entirely.
