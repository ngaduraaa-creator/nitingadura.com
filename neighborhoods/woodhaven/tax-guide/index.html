How I Explain Woodhaven's Taxes to My Clients

Property taxes in Woodhaven are calculated differently depending on whether you're in New York City (Class 1 residential properties assessed at 6% of market value, then taxed at the composite NYC rate). For a median-priced home in Woodhaven at $780,000, you're looking at roughly $6,900/year — about $575/month in your escrow payment.

Exemptions I Always Tell My Clients About

  • STAR Exemption: Basic STAR saves $300–$1,500/year for owner-occupants. Enhanced STAR for seniors 65+ saves even more. Apply at NYS Department of Taxation.
  • Veterans Exemption: Eligible veterans receive an assessed value reduction
  • Senior Citizen Exemption: Income-qualified seniors 65+ may get up to 50% reduction
  • Disabled Persons Exemption: For qualifying homeowners with disabilities

My advice: Apply for every exemption you qualify for on day one. Most clients leave $300–$1,200/year on the table by not applying.

Appealing Your Assessment

In NYC, Class 1 property owners can file with the NYC Tax Commission by March 15. Bring recent comparable sales data. If your home has been reassessed higher than market value supports, an appeal is worth pursuing.
