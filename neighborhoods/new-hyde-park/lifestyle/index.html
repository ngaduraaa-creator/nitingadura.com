Living in New Hyde Park, Queens — My Honest Lifestyle Guide

By Nitin Gadura — who actually knows this neighborhood · Updated 2026-04-20

Most neighborhood guides are written by people who've never spent a weekend in the place they're describing. I've worked in New Hyde Park for years. Here's what it's actually like to live here.

The Character of New Hyde Park

Herricks High School's 10/10 GreatSchools rating and 98% math proficiency make New Hyde Park Nassau County's top school-district value.

Herricks High School ranks #23 in New York State — one of only a handful of public schools in Nassau County to achieve a perfect 10/10 GreatSchools rating with 98% math proficiency.

Parks & Green Space

  • Lakeville Park
  • New Hyde Park Memorial Park
  • Multiple Village Greens

Source: NYC Parks Department

Local Landmarks

  • Herricks High School (#23 in NY State)
  • Marcus Avenue Retail Corridor
  • Mineola (Nassau County Seat, nearby)

Food & Culture

New Hyde Park reflects Queens' extraordinary cultural diversity in its restaurants, grocery stores, and community life. This is one of the most culinarily diverse counties in the United States — your neighbors are likely to represent dozens of countries and culinary traditions. The main commercial corridors have everything from halal butchers and South Asian grocers to Dominican bakeries and Italian-American delis.

Community Feel

Median household income of $142,927 and a population of 43,075 creates the critical mass for a full range of neighborhood services: libraries, community gardens, youth sports leagues, houses of worship, and block associations. New Hyde Park residents tend to stay — that longevity creates genuine community bonds.

Weekend in New Hyde Park

  • Lakeville Park — walking, cycling, picnics
  • Jamaica Avenue or main corridor shopping and dining
  • Quick trip to Rockaway Beach via A train (seasonal)
  • Aqueduct Racetrack (seasonal thoroughbred racing)
  • Jamaica Bay Wildlife Refuge — a national park minutes away

My Take

New Hyde Park is the kind of neighborhood where you know your neighbors. Where kids play outside. Where the local diner has been open since 1975. If you're looking for Manhattan-style nightlife, this isn't it. If you're looking for a real home in a real neighborhood with strong community ties — New Hyde Park delivers.
