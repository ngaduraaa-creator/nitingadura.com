About Ozone Park, Queens

Ozone Park is one of Southeast Queens' most established and culturally rich neighborhoods. Situated between Richmond Hill to the north and Howard Beach to the south, the community has historically been home to Italian-American families — and over the past three decades, it has become a vibrant hub for South Asian, Indo-Guyanese, Caribbean, and Punjabi communities who have built deep roots here. The neighborhood's character is one of tight-knit streets, family-owned businesses along Liberty Avenue and 101st Avenue, and a genuine sense of community that is increasingly rare in New York City.

Housing in Ozone Park consists primarily of detached and semi-detached 1- and 2-family brick homes built in the mid-20th century, offering the kind of space and privacy that is difficult to find at comparable price points in other NYC boroughs. The neighborhood's proximity to JFK Airport creates strong rental demand — both from airport employees and from families who value the transit access it brings. PS 97, PS 108, and IS 77 serve the neighborhood's students, and the Liberty Avenue commercial strip provides everyday amenities within walking distance.

For real estate investors, Ozone Park's 2-family homes are among the most sought-after assets in Southeast Queens. Rental income from the lower unit typically covers a significant portion of the mortgage, making these properties attractive to both owner-occupants and buy-and-hold investors. The neighborhood's stability, transit access, and cultural density make it one of the most reliable real estate markets in Queens.
