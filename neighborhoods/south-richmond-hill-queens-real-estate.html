About South Richmond Hill, Queens

South Richmond Hill (ZIP 11419) is an extension of the broader Richmond Hill community, sharing its predominantly South Asian, Punjabi, and Indo-Guyanese character while offering a slightly more affordable entry point into Southeast Queens homeownership. The neighborhood sits south of the J train's Jamaica Avenue corridor and stretches along Liberty Avenue and 101st Avenue — two of the most culturally vibrant commercial corridors in all of Queens.

Housing in South Richmond Hill consists primarily of 1- and 2-family semi-detached and detached brick homes with yards, driveways, and the kind of space that draws families who want more than a co-op can offer. The neighborhood has seen growing development activity along 101st Avenue, with new businesses, community organizations, and infrastructure improvements reflecting renewed investment in the area. PS 134 serves the neighborhood's elementary school students. The community here is tightly knit, with strong ties to nearby gurdwaras, mandirs, and South Asian businesses on Liberty Avenue.

For first-time homebuyers from South Asian or Guyanese backgrounds, South Richmond Hill offers one of the most accessible entry points in Southeast Queens — with homes starting in a price range that makes the dream of homeownership achievable for families who might otherwise be priced out of adjacent Ozone Park or Howard Beach.
