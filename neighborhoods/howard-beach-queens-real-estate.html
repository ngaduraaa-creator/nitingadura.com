About Howard Beach, Queens

Howard Beach is one of Queens' most distinctive and desirable neighborhoods — a tight-knit community built along the edge of Jamaica Bay, offering a suburban feel within New York City limits. The neighborhood has a proud Italian-American heritage dating back generations, with family-owned restaurants, social clubs, and a community identity that is deeply held. The A train and JFK AirTrain connection at Howard Beach station make it one of the best-transit neighborhoods in Southeast Queens despite its waterfront character.

Housing in Howard Beach consists largely of private and semi-private single-family homes — many with direct canal or bay access — as well as well-maintained detached brick homes on quiet residential streets. The neighborhood's exclusivity is reflected in its pricing: Howard Beach commands some of the higher price points in Southeast Queens, with waterfront properties and well-maintained homes regularly trading above $800,000. Ralph DeMarco Park along the bay waterfront provides community recreation space, and PS 207 (Howard Beach School) serves the neighborhood's children.

One important consideration for Howard Beach buyers is flood zone status. Portions of Howard Beach sit within FEMA-designated flood zones due to proximity to Jamaica Bay. This can require mandatory flood insurance for federally backed mortgages and affects both annual costs and long-term property resilience. Nitin Gadura helps buyers understand flood zone status, insurance implications, and how to evaluate properties with these considerations in mind — critical knowledge that separates an informed Howard Beach purchase from a costly surprise.

Howard Beach Real Estate FAQ

Parts of Howard Beach are in FEMA flood zones (AE and AO) due to Jamaica Bay proximity. Buyers should verify specific addresses at msc.fema.gov. Flood insurance may be required with a federally backed mortgage and can add $1,000–$3,000+ annually. Nitin helps buyers evaluate this before making an offer.
Average home prices in Howard Beach range from $700,000–$850,000 for standard single-family homes. Canal-facing and waterfront properties regularly exceed $900,000–$1.2M. The neighborhood has limited inventory, which supports strong pricing.
The A train serves Howard Beach/JFK Airport station with direct service to Manhattan. The JFK AirTrain connects from this station — making Howard Beach ideal for JFK employees, airline workers, and frequent travelers. Commute to Midtown Manhattan runs approximately 45–55 minutes.
Howard Beach is considered one of the safer and more community-oriented neighborhoods in Queens. Its Italian-American heritage brings a family-first culture, local parks, and a quiet residential environment. PS 207 and several parochial schools serve the area. The waterfront and Ralph DeMarco Park offer outdoor recreation within walking distance.
