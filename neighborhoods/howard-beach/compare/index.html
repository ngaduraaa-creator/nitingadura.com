Howard Beach vs South Ozone Park — Which One Is Right for You?

My honest comparison · By Nitin Gadura · Updated 2026-04-20

This is one of the most common questions I get: "Should I buy in Howard Beach or South Ozone Park?" I know both neighborhoods well — here's my honest take.

Choose Howard Beach If...

Howard Beach is Queens' hidden waterfront gem — Cape Cods and split-levels with canal access or Jamaica Bay views at prices below comparable Nassau County waterfront communities.

Best fit: Queens' Only Waterfront Community with AirTrain Access buyers who prioritize A Train (Howard Beach–JFK Airport), JFK AirTrain (Howard Beach Station) access and Frank M. Charles Memorial Park (Jamaica Bay waterfront).

Choose South Ozone Park If...

South Ozone Park offers newer housing stock than neighboring Woodhaven, JFK airport connectivity, and a tight-knit residential community — all below the Queens median price.

Best fit: Queens' 2-Family Investment Capital buyers.

My Bottom Line

I don't have a preference — I just want you in the right home for your family and budget. Some buyers I meet are clearly Howard Beach people; others belong in South Ozone Park. The only way to know is to walk both neighborhoods, see some homes, and talk it through. That's a conversation I'm happy to have.
