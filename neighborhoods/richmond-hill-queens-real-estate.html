About Richmond Hill, Queens

Richmond Hill is one of the most culturally vibrant and globally significant neighborhoods in all of New York City. Home to what is widely considered the largest Indo-Guyanese and Punjabi Sikh community in North America, Richmond Hill pulses with a cultural energy that is both deeply rooted and constantly evolving. Liberty Avenue — the neighborhood's main commercial artery — is lined with Guyanese and Caribbean restaurants, South Asian grocery stores, fabric shops, sari centers, and community businesses that serve a population of extraordinary diversity.

The housing stock in Richmond Hill consists predominantly of 2-family detached and semi-detached brick homes, which makes the neighborhood particularly attractive to buyers seeking both space and rental income. Many families purchase 2-family homes, live in one unit, and rent the other — a time-tested strategy in this community that has helped generation after generation build equity. The J train along Jamaica Avenue connects residents to Manhattan, and the neighborhood's central Queens location provides good access across the borough. PS 54, PS 55, and IS 238 serve the community's students.

Richmond Hill's numerous Sikh gurdwaras, Hindu mandirs, mosques, and Indo-Caribbean spiritual centers give the neighborhood a religious and community infrastructure that is rare in any American city. For South Asian, Guyanese, or Caribbean families, Richmond Hill offers something that goes beyond real estate: a true sense of home.
