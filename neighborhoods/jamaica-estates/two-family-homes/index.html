The Owner-Occupant Strategy (My Favorite)

Here's what I tell every first-time buyer in Jamaica Estates: buy a 2-family, live in one unit, rent the other. With FHA financing (3.5% down), you can qualify for a larger loan because the lender counts 75% of projected rental income. Your tenant helps pay your mortgage. In 3–5 years, many of my clients then buy a second property and rent out both units.

Pure Investment: What to Look For

For pure investment (non-owner-occupied) 2-family purchases, I look for:

  • Legal 2-family designation — verified with NYC DOF. Non-conforming 2-family homes create legal and insurance headaches.
  • Separate utility meters — tenants pay their own utilities; your operating costs drop significantly
  • Updated boiler and roof — avoids major capital expenditure in years 1–5
  • Tenants in place with rent history — immediate cash flow, known income
  • Rear parking or driveway — reduces tenant turnover in Queens

Financing 2-Family Investments

Investment property (non-owner-occupied): 25% down, 680+ credit score, 6 months reserves. Owner-occupied: FHA at 3.5% down, conventional at 5–10% down (with rental income qualifying help). I work with lenders who specialize in Queens multi-family — this matters for smooth closings.
