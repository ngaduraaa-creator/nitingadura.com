Common Questions

FAQ — Selling an Inherited House in Queens

Do I need to go through probate to sell an inherited house in Queens?

In most cases, yes. If the deceased did not have a living trust or joint tenancy arrangement, the estate must go through New York Surrogate's Court in Queens County. The executor named in the will (or administrator if there's no will) receives Letters Testamentary, which authorizes them to sell the property. This process typically takes 3–6 months in Queens before a sale can close.

What is the step-up in tax basis for an inherited property in NY?

When you inherit a property, the IRS "steps up" the cost basis to the fair market value on the date of the decedent's death. This means if your parent bought a home in Queens for $200,000 and it's worth $750,000 at death, your basis becomes $750,000. If you sell at that value, you likely owe zero capital gains tax. This is a major advantage compared to receiving property as a gift during the owner's lifetime.

What if multiple heirs disagree about selling the inherited Queens home?

If heirs cannot agree, any co-owner can petition the court for a partition action in NY Supreme Court, which can order the property sold and proceeds divided. However, partition actions are costly and time-consuming — often 1–2 years. In most cases, I work with all heirs together to help reach consensus on price and terms before it gets to that point.

How long does it take to sell an inherited house in Queens?

From the time you receive Letters Testamentary, the actual sale process (listing, offer, contract, closing) typically takes 60–90 days for a standard Queens home. Add 3–6 months for the Surrogate's Court process if not already complete. Queens homes are in strong demand and historically sell within 30–45 days of listing when priced correctly.

Do I need to make repairs before selling an inherited Queens home?

No. Inherited properties can be sold as-is. NY requires a Property Condition Disclosure Statement (PCDS) under RPL §462, but sellers can offer a $500 credit in lieu of completing it. As-is sales are common for estate properties in Queens, and many buyers specifically seek estate homes for the opportunity to renovate and build equity.
