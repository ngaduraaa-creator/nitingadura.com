Common Questions

FAQ — Selling a Queens Rental Property

Can I sell a tenant-occupied rental property in Queens?

Yes. You can sell a tenant-occupied property. Tenants have the right to remain through the end of their lease regardless of a sale — the new owner takes the property subject to the existing lease. You must provide proper notice for showings (typically 24 hours in NY), and the tenant's security deposit transfers to the buyer at closing. Some investors specifically prefer tenant-occupied properties for immediate income.

How is capital gains tax calculated when selling a Queens rental property?

Capital gains = sale price minus your adjusted cost basis (original purchase price + capital improvements minus depreciation taken). Depreciation recapture is taxed at 25% federal. Net gain above that is taxed at long-term rates (0%, 15%, or 20%). NY state also taxes the gain. A Queens rental bought for $400K with significant depreciation and sold for $800K would have substantial taxable gain — consult a CPA before listing.

What is a 1031 exchange and how does it apply to my Queens rental?

IRC Section 1031 allows you to defer capital gains taxes by reinvesting sale proceeds into a like-kind replacement property. You must identify a replacement within 45 days of closing and close on it within 180 days. The replacement must be of equal or greater value. I can connect you with a qualified intermediary before we list so you're ready to start the exchange clock properly at closing.

How do I handle tenant rights when selling my Queens rental?

Queens tenants have significant rights under NY law. You must honor all existing leases through their term. Month-to-month tenants require proper notice under NY RPL §226-c (30 days for under 1 year, 60 days for 1–2 years, 90 days for 2+ years). You cannot force out a tenant simply to sell — the buyer inherits the tenancy.

Will having a tenant make my Queens rental harder to sell?

Not necessarily. Tenant-occupied properties are often preferred by investors who want immediate income. A tenant paying market rent with a documented lease can actually be a selling point — it de-risks the purchase for an investor buyer who doesn't want vacancy while finding a renter.
