Common Questions

FAQ — Selling a Probate House in Queens

What is probate and when is it required to sell a house in Queens?

Probate is the legal process through which a deceased person's estate is administered under court supervision. In NY, probate is required to sell real property if the deceased was the sole owner and did not have a living trust. The case is filed in Queens County Surrogate's Court, which issues Letters Testamentary (with a will) or Letters of Administration (without a will) authorizing the executor or administrator to act.

How long does probate take in Queens County, NY?

An uncontested probate with a clear will typically takes 3–6 months in Queens County Surrogate's Court. Contested estates can take 1–3 years. The longer it takes, the more the estate pays in carrying costs on the vacant home — property taxes, insurance, maintenance. Filing promptly after death is important.

Can I list the house for sale before probate is complete?

In most cases, no — you cannot legally transfer title until the executor has Letters Testamentary. However, you can prepare the home, get a market valuation, and interview agents during the probate process. Once Letters are issued, I can move quickly to list. In some cases, an estate attorney can expedite court approval of a pending sale.

What are the executor's responsibilities when selling a probate property in NY?

The executor must: get an estate appraisal, pay all estate debts before distributing proceeds, file required estate tax returns (NY threshold is $7.16M in 2024), maintain the property during sale, and distribute remaining proceeds per the will or NY intestacy law. The executor has a fiduciary duty to act in the best interests of all beneficiaries.

Does the court need to approve the sale price for a probate property in Queens?

In standard NY probate, court confirmation of a specific sale price is not required — the executor has authority to sell at market value. However, if there are disputed beneficiaries, the court may review sale terms. A full MLS listing with competitive bidding is the best way to demonstrate market value and protect the executor from any later challenge.
