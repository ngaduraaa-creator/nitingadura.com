Selling Before Foreclosure Protects You in 4 Key Ways

  • Protect Your Credit Score A completed foreclosure damages your credit for 7 years. A pre-foreclosure sale (mortgage paid in full from proceeds) is a far lesser credit event.
  • Keep Your Equity Queens homes averaged ~$750K in 2024. If you have equity, a foreclosure auction is almost always the worst way to liquidate it. An MLS sale nets significantly more.
  • Control the Timeline In a foreclosure, the bank controls everything. With a voluntary sale, you set the move-out date and negotiate terms that work for your transition.
  • Avoid Deficiency Judgments If the foreclosure auction doesn't cover the full mortgage balance, lenders may pursue a deficiency judgment. A sale that pays off the mortgage in full eliminates this risk.

NY Foreclosure Timeline — What You Need to Know

Pre-foreclosure starts: After 90+ days of missed payments

Lis pendens filed: Public notice recorded against property title

NY is judicial: Lenders must sue in court — this gives you more time than non-judicial states

Timeline to auction: Typically 2–3 years in Queens County courts

Best window to sell: Immediately after lis pendens — before judgment

Common Questions

FAQ — Avoiding Foreclosure by Selling in Queens

What is pre-foreclosure and how long do I have to sell in NY?

Pre-foreclosure begins when your lender files a lis pendens after 90+ days of missed payments. New York is a judicial foreclosure state, meaning lenders must sue in court. The process can take 2–3 years in Queens County, giving you a window to sell. But act quickly — waiting reduces your options and can burn through your equity in mounting fees.

Can I sell my home after a lis pendens is filed in Queens?

Yes. A lis pendens does not prevent you from selling. It is recorded against the title and must be disclosed to buyers, but any sale proceeds will first pay off the outstanding mortgage and arrears. If equity remains after payoff, you receive it at closing. I can help you list quickly to maximize that equity before it erodes further.

How does a pre-foreclosure sale affect my credit?

A successful sale — even in pre-foreclosure — is far better than a completed foreclosure. A foreclosure stays on your credit report for 7 years and can drop your score 100–150+ points. A pre-foreclosure sale where the mortgage is paid in full reports as a satisfied mortgage, a significantly lesser credit impact.

What if I owe more than the home is worth?

A short sale may be an option — your lender agrees to accept less than what's owed. This requires lender approval and takes longer than a standard sale. However, Queens homes have appreciated significantly (~$750K avg in 2024), so many homeowners who think they're underwater actually have equity. I'll give you an honest assessment before we assume a short sale is needed.

How fast can you sell my Queens home to stop foreclosure?

Once listed and priced correctly, Queens homes typically receive offers within 1–4 weeks. A cash buyer can close in as little as 2–3 weeks from acceptance. A financed buyer typically closes in 45–60 days. I'll prioritize speed without sacrificing your net proceeds, and I can target cash buyers and investors if your timeline is extremely tight.
