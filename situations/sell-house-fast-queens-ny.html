Common Questions

FAQ — Selling Your Queens Home Fast

What is the average time to sell a house in Queens, NY?

Queens homes average 30–45 days on market before going under contract, with another 45–60 days to close after a signed contract. Total typical timeline from listing to closing: 75–105 days. With strategic pre-marketing and aggressive pricing, I regularly achieve signed contracts within 7–14 days of listing for well-positioned Queens properties.

How do you sell a Queens house faster than average?

The three biggest levers for a fast Queens sale: 1) Price at or just below market to generate multiple offers in the first week, 2) Pre-marketing — building buyer interest before the home hits MLS, 3) Making the home showing-ready from day one with professional photography. I deploy all three simultaneously for every listing.

Should I accept a cash offer to sell faster?

Cash offers close faster (typically 2–4 weeks vs. 45–60 days for financed buyers) and have fewer contingencies. However, cash buyers often offer 5–15% below market. In a competitive Queens market, I prefer to list on MLS and create competition among multiple buyers including cash offers — this typically nets more than accepting the first cash offer directly.

Does selling fast mean getting less money in Queens?

Not necessarily. A correctly priced home that generates multiple offers in the first week can sell fast AND at full asking price or above. The key: 'fast' should come from strong pricing strategy and marketing, not from slashing the price. I'll price your home to sell within 7–21 days while still achieving market value or better through competitive buyer interest.

What is pre-marketing and how does it help sell faster?

Pre-marketing is building buyer interest before a home officially hits MLS. I use my buyer list, social media, agent-to-agent networking, and targeted online ads to generate showings before the official listing date. When the home goes live, there's already a pool of motivated buyers ready to view — compressing the time to first offer significantly.
