Common Questions

FAQ — Selling a Queens Home With a Tax Lien

Can I sell my Queens house if it has a property tax lien?

Yes. A property tax lien does not prevent a sale. At closing, the title company obtains a payoff from the lien holder and pays it from the sale proceeds. The buyer receives clear title. As long as the sale price covers the mortgage, tax lien, and closing costs, you can sell with a lien in place.

How does the NYC tax lien sale work in Queens?

NYC conducts an annual tax lien sale in which unpaid property tax debt is sold to private investors. Once sold, the lien accrues interest at rates up to 18% per year. If the lien goes unpaid and reaches foreclosure (which can take several years), you lose the property. Selling before lien foreclosure is critical to preserving any equity.

How do I find out the exact payoff amount for my Queens tax lien?

If the lien is still held by NYC, request a payoff through the NYC Department of Finance website or by calling 311. If the lien has been sold to a private investor, their contact information is on your DOF property tax bill or lien notice. Your real estate attorney will obtain a formal payoff letter as part of the closing process.

Does a tax lien affect what buyers will pay for my Queens home?

No. A tax lien does not reduce market value from a buyer's perspective — they're buying free and clear of the lien (it's paid at closing). The lien reduces your net proceeds, not the sale price. The market values your home based on location and condition, not your lien status.

What if the tax lien exceeds my equity — can I still sell?

If your mortgage balance plus tax lien exceeds the home's market value, you'd need a short sale (lender and/or lien holder accepts less) or bring cash to closing to bridge the gap. Queens homes have appreciated significantly (~$750K avg 2024), so many homeowners have more equity than they realize even with a lien. I'll give you an honest assessment upfront.
