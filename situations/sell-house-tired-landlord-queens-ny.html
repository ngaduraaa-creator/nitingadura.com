Common Questions

FAQ — Tired Landlord Selling a Queens Rental

How do I sell my Queens rental property with a difficult tenant?

You can sell with a tenant in place — the buyer takes ownership subject to the existing tenancy. You cannot legally remove a tenant simply to sell. If the tenant is month-to-month, you can provide required notice (30–90 days per NY RPL §226-c) but they aren't required to leave until after the notice period. I target investor buyers who prefer occupied properties for immediate rental income.

Is it worth evicting a tenant before selling my Queens rental?

NY eviction proceedings are long and expensive — even uncontested holdovers can take 6–12 months and $5,000–$15,000+ in legal fees. Selling with the tenant in place to an investor buyer is usually faster and cheaper. Owner-occupants may pay slightly more for a vacant property, but the math often favors selling occupied when eviction would take many months.

Can I do a 1031 exchange when I sell my Queens rental as a tired landlord?

Yes. If you've held the property as an investment, it qualifies for a 1031 exchange. The key is choosing a replacement that requires less active management: a triple-net (NNN) commercial property, a Delaware Statutory Trust (DST), or a well-managed multi-family with professional property management. The 1031 lets you defer capital gains while eliminating day-to-day landlord responsibilities.

How do I calculate my capital gains exposure when selling my Queens rental?

Capital gain = sale price minus adjusted cost basis (original price + improvements minus depreciation). Depreciation recapture is taxed at 25% federal. Long-term gains are taxed at 0%, 15%, or 20% depending on income, plus NY state taxes. For a Queens rental held 10+ years, the gain can be very substantial. Consult a CPA and 1031 intermediary before listing.

Who buys tenant-occupied Queens rental properties?

Active investors, portfolio landlords, and 1031 exchange buyers specifically prefer tenant-occupied Queens rentals. Occupied properties mean immediate rental income and no vacancy risk between ownership. I maintain relationships with qualified Queens investors and 1031 buyers who seek rental properties and can close efficiently.
