Common Questions

FAQ — Selling a Queens Home During Divorce

How does NY equitable distribution affect selling a house during divorce?

New York is an equitable distribution state — marital property is divided fairly, though not necessarily 50/50. Courts consider the length of the marriage, each spouse's income, and contributions to the home. Most couples choose to sell and split proceeds rather than one spouse buying out the other, since this provides a clean financial separation.

What if one spouse refuses to sell the Queens home?

If one spouse refuses to cooperate, the court can appoint a referee to complete the sale. In contested divorces, a judge can issue a forced sale order under NY law. Working with a neutral agent early almost always avoids this costly step — a referee sale may net both spouses less than a properly marketed MLS sale.

Can we sell the house before the divorce is finalized in NY?

Yes. You do not need to wait for a final divorce decree. Both spouses must agree and sign all sale documents. If you have a separation agreement, it can specify how proceeds are held in escrow and distributed at final judgment. Selling before finalization can also preserve the $500K capital gains exclusion for married couples filing jointly.

How do I handle capital gains tax when selling a home during divorce?

The IRS allows a $500,000 capital gains exclusion for married couples selling a primary residence (IRC Section 121). If you sell before the divorce is finalized and are still filing jointly, you may qualify for the full $500K exclusion. After divorce, each person gets only the $250K individual exclusion. Timing can save significant taxes — consult a CPA for your specific situation.

How quickly can we sell a Queens home during a divorce?

Queens homes typically sell within 30–45 days of listing when priced at market value. If both spouses cooperate, I can often have a signed contract within 3–4 weeks of listing. Mortgage payoff, agent commissions, transfer taxes, and attorney fees are all handled at the closing table before proceeds are distributed.
