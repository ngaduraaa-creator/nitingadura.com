Common Questions

FAQ — Selling a Queens Condo

What is a resale package and why do I need it to sell my Queens condo?

A resale package is a set of documents buyers' attorneys request from your condo association before closing — current HOA budget, meeting minutes, reserve fund balance, pending special assessments, rules and bylaws, and a statement of any liens or dues on your unit. Many associations charge $200–$500 to prepare it. Request it early — it can take 2–4 weeks to receive.

What is FIRPTA and does it apply to my Queens condo sale?

FIRPTA (26 U.S.C. §1445) requires buyers to withhold 15% of the sale price from a foreign seller and remit it to the IRS. If you are a US citizen or resident alien, FIRPTA does not apply. If you are a non-resident foreign seller, your attorney must handle the withholding certificate process. This is handled at closing.

Do condos have board approval requirements like co-ops in Queens?

Most Queens condos do not require board approval of the specific buyer. However, some condo associations have a right of first refusal — they can buy your unit at the sale price before you sell to a third party. Check your condo declaration and bylaws. Condo closings are generally faster than co-ops because there's no board approval waiting period.

How do condo HOA dues affect my sale?

High HOA dues can affect buyer affordability — lenders include dues in debt-to-income calculations. At closing, any unpaid dues are paid from your proceeds. Any special assessments outstanding or upcoming must be disclosed to buyers. I'll help you contextualize your dues against comparable buildings to position the sale favorably.

How long does a condo sale take in Queens compared to a co-op?

Condo sales typically close faster than co-op sales. Without board approval requirements, the timeline from accepted offer to closing is typically 45–60 days for a financed buyer and 20–30 days for a cash buyer — 1–2 months faster than a comparable co-op transaction.
