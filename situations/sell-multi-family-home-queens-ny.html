Common Questions

FAQ — Selling a Queens Multi-Family Home

How is a multi-family home valued differently than a single-family in Queens?

Multi-family homes are valued using both the comparable sales approach and the income approach (GRM or cap rate analysis). Investor buyers care most about income potential: current rents, potential market rents, and operating expenses. Queens 2-family GRMs typically range 12–18x, with cap rates of 4–7%. I'll run both analyses and price to attract the widest qualified buyer pool.

What are tenant rights when I sell my Queens multi-family?

Tenants must remain through the end of their lease term regardless of who owns the building. Month-to-month tenants require advance notice per NY RPL §226-c (30–90 days depending on tenancy length). Rent-stabilized tenants have additional protections. The buyer inherits all tenancies — I'll make sure this is clearly disclosed in the listing.

Who buys multi-family homes in Queens?

Three buyer pools: 1) owner-occupant buyers (live in one unit, rent others — FHA eligible with 3.5% down), 2) local investors building portfolios, and 3) 1031 exchange buyers reinvesting from other properties. I market to all three simultaneously to maximize competition and the ultimate sale price.

What is a Gross Rent Multiplier (GRM) and cap rate for Queens multi-families?

GRM = sale price divided by annual gross rent. Queens 2–4 family GRMs typically range 12–18x depending on neighborhood and condition. Cap rate = net operating income divided by sale price; Queens multi-family cap rates typically range 4–7%. I'll calculate both for your property so buyers and lenders have the analysis they need.

Can owner-occupant buyers purchase a Queens multi-family with a low down payment?

Yes. FHA loans allow owner-occupant buyers to purchase 2–4 unit properties with as little as 3.5% down (580+ credit score). Conventional loans also offer owner-occupant 2–4 family financing with lower down payments than pure investment purchases. This significantly expands your buyer pool and can support a higher sale price than investor-only competition.
