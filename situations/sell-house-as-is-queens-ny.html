Common Questions

FAQ — Selling a Queens Home As-Is

What does selling "as-is" mean in New York?

Selling as-is means the seller will make no repairs and the buyer accepts the property in its current condition. Under NY RPL §462, sellers must complete a Property Condition Disclosure Statement (PCDS) OR offer the buyer a $500 credit in lieu of the disclosure. As-is does NOT exempt you from disclosing known material defects — those must still be disclosed.

Will I get less money selling my Queens home as-is?

Typically, yes — buyers price in the cost of repairs they'll need to make. However, the gap between as-is and renovated prices is often smaller than sellers expect, especially in Queens where renovation costs are high. If $50K in repairs only adds $40K to the sale price, selling as-is actually nets more. I'll analyze the math honestly with you.

What kinds of buyers purchase Queens homes as-is?

Investors who flip or rent, contractors seeking a project, and buyers who want to renovate to their own taste are all active in Queens. Cash investors can close in 2–4 weeks without inspection contingencies. Conventional buyers can also buy as-is with inspections but won't ask for repairs. I market to both pools to maximize competition.

Do I have to disclose defects when selling as-is in NY?

Yes. The as-is label does not exempt you from disclosing known material defects. You must either complete the PCDS (detailing known conditions of roof, foundation, plumbing, electrical, HVAC, etc.) or pay the buyer the $500 statutory credit. Failing to disclose known defects can expose you to post-closing liability regardless of any as-is language in the contract.

Should I clean up and declutter before an as-is sale?

Yes — even an as-is sale benefits from being clean and uncluttered. Buyers struggle to see past clutter, which leads to lower offers. A thorough cleanout and basic cleaning (no painting or repairs needed) lets buyers visualize the property's true potential. Clean homes photograph better and attract stronger offers even when sold as-is.
