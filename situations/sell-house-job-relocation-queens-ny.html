Common Questions

FAQ — Relocation Home Sales in Queens

How fast can you sell my Queens home if I'm relocating for a job?

Queens homes priced at market value typically receive offers within 7–21 days and close within 30–60 days thereafter. If you need to move in 6–8 weeks, that's achievable with the right pricing and marketing approach. For extremely tight timelines, I can target cash buyers who can close in as little as 2–3 weeks from contract.

Can I close on my Queens home remotely if I've already moved?

Yes. NY closings can be done remotely via Power of Attorney. You would execute a POA authorizing a trusted person (attorney, family member) to sign on your behalf at the closing table. Some attorneys also offer remote online notarization (RON) for certain documents. You don't need to be physically present in Queens to close.

What is a bridge loan and do I need one when relocating?

A bridge loan is a short-term loan that lets you use your Queens home's equity to purchase a new home before your Queens property closes. This is useful if you need to buy in your destination city before the Queens sale completes. Bridge loans have higher rates than regular mortgages but provide flexibility. If you can rent temporarily in the new city, that's often simpler and cheaper.

Should I sell or rent my Queens home when relocating?

This depends on your financial situation, how long you plan to stay in the new city, and your appetite for being a long-distance landlord. Selling provides a clean break and liquidity. Renting preserves the asset but requires managing a tenant from out of state. Queens rents are strong — median 2BR exceeds $2,500/month in many neighborhoods — so renting is viable if you're not pressed for cash.

Will my employer cover closing costs for a relocation sale?

Some employers offer relocation packages that include a portion of real estate agent commissions and closing costs. Ask HR about your company's relocation assistance policy before listing. If your employer offers a guaranteed purchase program (they buy your home directly), compare that offer against a traditional MLS sale — MLS often yields a significantly higher net.
