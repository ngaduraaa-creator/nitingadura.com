Selling Guide

Queens Home Selling Timeline: From Listing to Closing

A week-by-week breakdown of what actually happens when you sell a home in Queens — including the steps most sellers don't know about until they're in the middle of them.

The average Queens home sale takes 10–16 weeks from first conversation to closing day. That's a realistic number — not a best-case scenario. Sellers who understand the timeline in advance move through it with far less stress and make better decisions at every stage.

Here's what each phase actually involves, with realistic timeframes based on current Queens market conditions.

Phase 1: Preparation (Weeks 1–4)

Week1

Initial Consultation & CMA

We meet at your property. I conduct a Comparative Market Analysis (CMA) using recent closed sales within 0.5 miles — typically 6–10 comparable properties. We discuss pricing strategy, your timeline, and any improvements that could affect value. This meeting takes 60–90 minutes.

Week1–2

Hire Your Real Estate Attorney

New York is an attorney state — you must have an attorney to complete a real estate transaction. I'll refer you to experienced Queens real estate attorneys if you need one. Typical fee: $1,500–$3,000. Get them engaged early so they're ready when a contract needs to be drafted.

Weeks2–4

Prep, Repairs & Staging

Declutter every room (buyers need to see the space, not your belongings). Make targeted repairs: leaky faucets, broken fixtures, scuffed baseboards, sticking doors. A well-prepped home sells faster and for more money. Professional staging is worth considering for homes over $700K. At minimum, deep clean and improve curb appeal.

Week3–4

Gather Documents

Locate: deed, survey, prior title report, Certificate of Occupancy, any open permits, utility bills (12 months), property tax bills, HOA documents (if applicable), co-op proprietary lease and financial statements (if applicable). Missing documents slow closings significantly.

Phase 2: Listing & Showings (Weeks 3–8)

Week3–4

Professional Photography & MLS Entry

Professional photos are non-negotiable in Queens. Homes with high-quality photos receive significantly more online views. I arrange photography, write the MLS listing, and prepare supplemental marketing materials. Your home goes live on the MLS, Zillow, StreetEasy, Realtor.com, and syndicated platforms simultaneously.

Weeks4–8

Showings, Open Houses & Feedback

Expect most buyer activity in the first 14–21 days. I conduct showings 7 days a week including evenings. After each showing, I collect buyer agent feedback and report it to you weekly. If showing activity is high but no offers, we discuss price. If showing traffic is low, we discuss marketing adjustments. In a well-priced Queens market, offers typically come within 3–5 weeks.

Queens buyer pool note: Queens has one of the most diverse buyer pools in the nation. In neighborhoods like Ozone Park, Richmond Hill, and Howard Beach, a significant portion of buyers are South Asian, Indo-Caribbean, or Italian-American families who often shop in specific communities, use specific agents, and make decisions on different timescales than the national average. Targeted marketing in these communities matters.

Phase 3: Offer & Contract (Weeks 5–10)

Week5–8

Receiving and Negotiating Offers

When offers come in, I present each one with full context: offer price, financing type (conventional, FHA, cash), proposed closing date, contingencies (mortgage, inspection, appraisal), and buyer pre-approval status. We respond strategically — counter, accept, or request highest-and-best if multiple offers arrive. I advise on which offers carry the least risk, not just the highest price.

Week6–9

Attorney Review & Contract Execution

Once we accept an offer, your attorney drafts the purchase contract (or reviews the buyer's attorney's draft). Both attorneys negotiate terms: price adjustments, what personal property is included, closing date, contingency terms. This process typically takes 1–3 weeks. The contract becomes binding when both parties sign. The buyer typically delivers 10% earnest money at contract signing.

Phase 4: Due Diligence (Weeks 8–14)

Weeks8–10

Home Inspection

The buyer orders a home inspection (their cost, typically $450–$700). The inspector has 2–4 hours inside the property. You do not attend. The buyer may then request repairs or credits. I advise you on which requests are reasonable, which to reject, and how to negotiate effectively. Major issues (structural, roof, electrical) require careful handling. Minor cosmetic items are typically your choice to address or not.

Weeks8–12

Appraisal (If Buyer Is Financing)

The buyer's lender orders an appraisal to confirm the property value supports the loan. If the appraisal comes in below the purchase price, the parties must negotiate: seller reduces price, buyer makes up the difference in cash, or deal falls apart. In Queens, appraisals can be challenging in fast-moving neighborhoods — having comparable sales data ready is critical.

Weeks8–14

Mortgage Contingency Period (30–45 Days)

The buyer has 30–45 days from contract signing to obtain a mortgage commitment letter. If they cannot secure financing within this window, they can cancel the contract and recover their deposit. Once the commitment letter is received, the mortgage contingency is satisfied and the sale moves toward closing.

Weeks8–16

Co-op Board Approval (If Applicable — Adds 4–8 Weeks)

If you're selling a co-op, the buyer must be approved by the board. The buyer prepares a board package (financial statements, personal references, employment letters, bank statements). The board reviews it, schedules an interview, and votes to approve or reject. Queens co-op boards vary widely — some are straightforward; others are strict about debt-to-income ratios, subletting plans, or requiring significant liquid assets post-closing.

Phase 5: Closing (Weeks 10–16)

Week12–16

Clear to Close & Final Walkthrough

Once the lender issues a "clear to close," closing is typically scheduled 5–7 business days out. The buyer does a final walkthrough (24–48 hours before closing) to confirm the property's condition matches the contract terms and that agreed repairs were completed. Be sure the home is in move-out condition at walkthrough — issues here can delay closing.

Week13–16

Closing Day

Closing in Queens takes place at the title company's office or a law office, typically with both attorneys present. You sign a deed, transfer documents, and various state and city tax forms. Your attorney handles disbursements from the closing proceeds — paying off your mortgage, paying transfer taxes, and wiring your net proceeds. Plan for 2–4 hours. You'll receive your net proceeds same day by wire or certified check.

Realistic Total Timeline Summary

  • Preparation: 2–4 weeks
  • On market to accepted offer: 2–6 weeks (priced-right homes, often less)
  • Contract execution: 1–3 weeks
  • Inspection, appraisal, mortgage: 4–6 weeks
  • Co-op board approval (if applicable): +4–8 weeks
  • Closing preparation: 1–2 weeks
  • Total (non co-op): 10–16 weeks
  • Total (co-op): 14–24 weeks

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to sell a house in Queens, NY?

From the time you list your home, the average Queens sale takes 30–60 days to go into contract, then another 45–60 days to close — for a total of roughly 10–16 weeks start to finish. Co-op sales take longer due to board approval, which adds 4–8 weeks.

What happens during attorney review in a Queens home sale?

In New York, both buyer and seller are represented by attorneys who negotiate and execute the purchase contract. Attorney review typically takes 1–3 weeks. Your attorney will negotiate price adjustments, contingencies, and the closing date. Once both parties sign, the contract is binding.

How long does mortgage contingency last in a Queens sale?

The standard mortgage contingency period in Queens is 30–45 days from contract execution. If the buyer cannot obtain financing within that period, they can walk away and recover their down payment. The contingency can be extended by mutual agreement.

How long does co-op board approval take in Queens?

Co-op board approval in Queens typically takes 4–8 weeks after the buyer submits a complete board package. This includes document preparation (1–2 weeks), board review and scheduling an interview (2–4 weeks), and formal approval (1–2 weeks). Some boards meet monthly, which can extend the timeline.

What do I need to do to prepare my Queens home for sale?

Plan 2–4 weeks for preparation: declutter and deep clean, make minor repairs (leaky faucets, scuffed walls, broken fixtures), consider a pre-listing inspection, do light staging, and gather documents (deed, survey, CO certificates, utility bills). Well-prepared homes sell faster and for more money.
