About Springfield Gardens (11413)

Springfield Gardens is a residential enclave in Southeast Queens with a predominantly Caribbean and South Asian population that brings warmth and community identity to every block. The neighborhood sits just northeast of JFK International Airport, making it highly accessible for residents who commute within the city or travel frequently. The housing stock runs primarily to semi-detached brick and frame homes on quiet, tree-lined streets — the kind of solid, well-maintained properties that attract multigenerational families looking for space and stability at a price still under the Queens median.

Transit options are solid: the A train at Linden Boulevard and Springfield Boulevard connects riders to Manhattan in under an hour, and the Q113 and other local bus routes fill in last-mile gaps to JFK and other parts of the borough. For drivers, the Belt Parkway is minutes away, opening up Nassau County and Brooklyn. Schools serving the zip include PS 132 and PS 137, along with several parochial options, and the area is within district reach of some strong high schools.

The neighborhood's appeal has grown steadily over the past several years. Crime metrics have improved, new small businesses have opened along Merrick Boulevard and Springfield Boulevard, and homeowners who purchased in the early 2010s are sitting on meaningful equity. For buyers, it remains a market where you can still get a single-family or two-family home at a price that makes sense — a vanishingly rare thing in New York City in 2024.

Frequently Asked Questions — Springfield Gardens 11413

Is Springfield Gardens a good neighborhood to buy in 2024?

Yes — 11413 offers solid value relative to other Queens zip codes. Prices have appreciated steadily without the extreme volatility seen in more central neighborhoods, and the proximity to JFK, the A train, and major roadways makes it attractive to both owner-occupants and investors. Inventory remains tight, which supports prices.

What types of homes are most common in Springfield Gardens?

The dominant housing types are semi-detached single-family and two-family brick homes, typically built in the 1940s–1960s. You'll find some detached colonials on larger lots, especially near Springfield Park. Co-ops and condos are less common here compared to Forest Hills or Jamaica.

How long does it take to get to Manhattan from 11413?

The A train from Linden Blvd/Springfield Blvd reaches Fulton Street in lower Manhattan in roughly 45–55 minutes, and Midtown (42nd Street–Port Authority) in about 55–65 minutes. The Far Rockaway branch of the A is the relevant one — check direction before boarding.

Are there flood zone concerns in Springfield Gardens?

Parts of 11413 sit in or near FEMA flood zones, particularly blocks closer to the marshland near JFK and Spring Creek. Before purchasing, always verify the specific block's flood zone designation through NYC's official flood zone maps and confirm flood insurance requirements with your mortgage lender.
