About Ozone Park (11416)

Ozone Park is Nitin Gadura's home neighborhood — and home base for Gadura Real Estate LLC, located at 106-09 101st Ave. That is not a marketing point. It means that when Nitin tells you he knows this neighborhood block by block, he means it literally. He grew up in and around 11416, has watched it evolve over two decades, and has personal relationships throughout the community that give his clients a genuine information edge.

The neighborhood is a rich blend of Guyanese, Indo-Caribbean, South Asian, and Italian communities that have coexisted for generations. Liberty Avenue and Lefferts Boulevard form the commercial spine — halal butchers, Guyanese roti shops, Italian delis, and South Asian grocers all within a short walk of each other. The housing stock runs primarily to semi-detached and attached brick homes on well-maintained lots, with a significant two-family home presence that reflects the multigenerational living preferences of many community residents. The A train on Lefferts Boulevard provides subway access to downtown Manhattan, JFK Airport is five minutes by car or bus, and the Belt Parkway connects to Brooklyn and Nassau County quickly.

Ozone Park's proximity to JFK creates strong rental demand — a meaningful factor for investor buyers and for owners seeking to leverage accessory apartments. PS 97, PS 108, and several parochial schools serve the neighborhood. Inventory has historically been moderate in 11416, with homes routinely receiving multiple offers from both local move-up buyers and investors from the broader Queens market. For sellers, this is one of the more competitive buyer environments in Southeast Queens.

Frequently Asked Questions — Ozone Park 11416

Is Ozone Park a good investment for rental properties?

Yes — Ozone Park's proximity to JFK Airport creates persistent rental demand from airline workers, travelers, and new arrivals to the US who want a community feel with airport proximity. Two-family homes in 11416 are a particularly popular vehicle for investors because the rental income from the upper or lower unit can substantially offset carrying costs. Gross rental yields in 11416 typically run 5–7% depending on purchase price and unit configuration.

What is the A train commute like from Ozone Park?

The A train at Lefferts Blvd provides access to the Far Rockaway and Rockaway Park branches, with express service to Manhattan. The trip to Jay Street–MetroTech in Downtown Brooklyn takes about 25 minutes, and to Fulton Center in Lower Manhattan about 35 minutes. Midtown (42nd St) is roughly 45–55 minutes. Service is frequent during peak hours and reliable enough for daily commuting.

Are there new developments happening in Ozone Park?

11416 is not a development hotspot like Long Island City or Jamaica, but there is steady infill development — primarily new two-family and three-family homes on lots where older structures have been demolished. This adds newer inventory to the market but does not meaningfully disrupt the neighborhood's character, which remains predominantly homeowning and residential.
