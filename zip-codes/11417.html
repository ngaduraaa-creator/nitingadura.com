About Woodhaven/Ozone Park Border (11417)

Zip code 11417 straddles the boundary between Woodhaven and Ozone Park — two adjacent neighborhoods that share more in common than they differ. The result is a genuinely diverse, working-class community that attracts buyers seeking solid value and solid bones in their home. Jamaica Avenue, which runs along the northern edge of the zip, provides J and Z train access and a dense retail strip that keeps everyday errands convenient. Forest Park, one of Queens' largest and most underrated green spaces, borders the north and provides hiking trails, athletic fields, and a genuine sense of breathing room that you don't get in denser parts of the borough.

The housing stock is characterized by attached and semi-detached brick homes, most built in the 1920s through 1950s, many of which remain in their original configuration or have been sensitively updated. Two-family homes are common, and the neighborhood's affordability relative to more central Queens zip codes — combined with J/Z train access and the Belt Parkway via Woodhaven Blvd — makes it attractive to both first-time buyers and investors. The community includes significant South Asian, Latino, Eastern European, and multigenerational working-class families who bring stability and local investment to the neighborhood.

Commercial activity on Jamaica Avenue includes a mix of long-running local shops and an increasing number of new businesses drawn by the foot traffic from J/Z train riders. The Liberty Avenue corridor along the southern part of the zip provides additional retail and dining options, overlapping with the Richmond Hill and Ozone Park commercial ecosystems. For buyers priced out of Ozone Park proper (11416), 11417 often offers similar character at a slight discount.

Frequently Asked Questions — 11417 (Woodhaven/Ozone Park)

How is the J/Z train commute from 11417 to Manhattan?

The J and Z trains run along Jamaica Avenue with multiple stops in 11417. The J train provides local service to Downtown Brooklyn (Fulton St) in about 30–35 minutes and Lower Manhattan (Broad St) in about 40 minutes. The Z train runs during peak rush hours as a skip-stop express, shaving 5–8 minutes off the trip. It's a solid commute for Downtown workers, though less direct for Midtown riders who may need to transfer.

What is Forest Park like for residents of 11417?

Forest Park is one of Queens' most valuable residential amenities — 538 acres of Oak Forest, hiking and biking trails, athletic fields, a band shell for summer concerts, and the Forest Park Golf Course. For residents of 11417, it is essentially a backyard wilderness within walking distance. The park is far less crowded than Prospect Park or Central Park, making it genuinely usable for daily exercise year-round.

Is 11417 priced differently than neighboring 11416 (Ozone Park)?

Yes — 11417 typically prices slightly below 11416, primarily because Ozone Park proper has a denser buyer market and slightly stronger community name recognition. The practical differences in housing stock and neighborhood character are minimal for most of the overlapping zip boundary. Buyers priced out of 11416 often find comparable homes in 11417 at a 3–6% discount, which adds up meaningfully on a $700K purchase.

What schools serve zip code 11417?

Students in 11417 are served by a mix of Queens elementary, middle, and high schools. Key schools include PS 65 (Woodhaven), as well as nearby middle and high school options throughout District 27. Parochial options are also available in the area. As with any Queens zip, individual block assignments can vary — confirm your specific school assignment with NYC DOE using your address.
