Hempstead, Nassau County — Affordable Long Island Value

Hempstead (ZIP 11550) is the most populous incorporated village in New York State and sits in the heart of Nassau County. For Queens buyers priced out of the borough, Hempstead offers a compelling alternative: detached single-family homes, larger lots, and lower price points than most of Long Island — while still offering LIRR commuter access into Penn Station (approximately 45 minutes).

The community is predominantly Latino (Central American, Caribbean) and African American, with a growing South Asian presence. The housing stock consists mainly of post-WWII Cape Cods, ranches, and colonials — solid construction with good bones. Many investors purchase 2-family homes here for strong rental yields.

School districts in Hempstead are important to research — the Hempstead Union Free School District has historically faced challenges, while neighboring districts like Uniondale and Valley Stream offer stronger options for families. Always verify school zone boundaries when buying in 11550.

Hempstead Real Estate — Common Questions

How do Hempstead home prices compare to Queens?

Hempstead is typically 10–20% more affordable than comparable Queens zip codes, making it attractive for buyers who want more space for their budget. The trade-off is typically higher property taxes — Nassau County taxes average significantly higher than NYC taxes, so factor that into your monthly payment calculation.

What are Nassau County property taxes like in 11550?

Nassau County has some of the highest property taxes in the US. In Hempstead, expect annual taxes of $8,000–$14,000+ depending on the home. You can file a tax grievance annually if you believe your assessment is too high — many homeowners successfully reduce their taxes this way.

Is Hempstead a good place to invest in rental property?

Yes — strong rental demand, relatively affordable purchase prices, and an under-supplied housing market make Hempstead worth considering for investors. Two-family homes are especially popular. Just account for the higher property tax burden in your cash-flow analysis.

How long does it take to buy a home in Hempstead?

From accepted offer to closing typically takes 60–75 days in Nassau County (slightly longer than NYC due to the mortgage contingency period and title process). Having mortgage pre-approval ready before you make an offer significantly speeds things up.
