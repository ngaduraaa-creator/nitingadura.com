About Howard Beach (11414)

Howard Beach is one of Queens' most distinctly character-driven neighborhoods — a tight-knit, working-class community with deep Italian-American roots and a residential atmosphere that feels far removed from the urban density of the rest of the borough. Bordering Jamaica Bay to the south, the neighborhood offers canal-front and waterfront homes that are genuinely rare in New York City at any price point, making 11414 a sought-after destination for buyers who want suburban character with city access.

The housing stock is diverse, ranging from modest single-family colonials on small lots to substantial waterfront homes on private canals, some with private docks. The A train at Howard Beach–JFK Airport station puts Manhattan within reach, and JFK Airport itself is literally adjacent, making this neighborhood particularly attractive for airline employees and frequent flyers. The Belt Parkway runs along the northern edge of the neighborhood, connecting drivers to Brooklyn, Nassau County, and the Rockaways in minutes. PS 207 Howard Beach School and other local schools serve the community, and the neighborhood has a strong reputation for civic engagement.

Buyers considering Howard Beach should know that flood zone awareness is essential. Significant portions of 11414 — particularly blocks near the canals and Jamaica Bay — sit in FEMA flood zones, which means mandatory flood insurance for most financed purchases. Post-Hurricane Sandy, many homeowners elevated their structures or invested in flood mitigation, and this affects both the insurance cost profile and the long-term value of specific properties. That said, the waterfront lifestyle and the overall neighborhood appeal continue to command a premium, and demand remains healthy.

Flood Zone Notice

Frequently Asked Questions — Howard Beach 11414

What is the flood insurance situation in Howard Beach?

This is one of the most important due diligence items for any Howard Beach buyer. Many properties require federally-backed flood insurance (through the NFIP) as a mortgage condition, with annual premiums ranging from roughly $800 to over $5,000 depending on the property's elevation certificate, flood zone designation, and coverage level. Always request an elevation certificate for any canal or bay-adjacent property, and get a flood insurance quote before finalizing your offer budget.

How is the A train commute from Howard Beach to Manhattan?

The Howard Beach–JFK Airport A train station connects to Manhattan with express service. Midtown (34th Street–Penn Station) is approximately 50–60 minutes; Downtown Brooklyn (Jay St) is about 30 minutes. Service frequency is solid during peak hours, though late-night service can be slower. Riders can also catch the AirTrain from the same station to JFK Terminal connections.

Are there canal-front homes with private docks available in 11414?

Yes — Howard Beach is one of the few Queens neighborhoods where private canal-front homes with boat docks exist. These properties are rare and highly sought-after, typically priced 25–40% above comparable interior lots. Availability is extremely limited; working with an agent who knows when these listings are coming before they hit the public market is a significant advantage.

Is Howard Beach a good place to raise a family?

Howard Beach has long been considered a family-friendly community. The neighborhood is quiet and residential, crime rates are comparatively low, and there is a strong sense of community among long-term residents. PS 207 is the local elementary school, and the neighborhood feeds into several Queens high schools. Proximity to the Belt Parkway and JFK makes weekend travel and logistics easy for families.
