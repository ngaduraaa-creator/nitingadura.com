About Kew Gardens (11415)

Kew Gardens is a quiet, historically rich neighborhood in central Queens that has long attracted professionals, academics, and families seeking a calmer residential environment without sacrificing transit convenience. The E and F express trains at Union Turnpike–Kew Gardens station make it possible to reach Midtown Manhattan in about 35–40 minutes — a commute time that would be envied in most other parts of the borough. This combination of neighborhood calm and transit speed makes 11415 consistently competitive despite its relatively higher price point.

The housing stock is a compelling mix of attached brick homes from the 1920s and 1930s, mid-century co-ops with well-run boards, and a handful of detached colonials on more generous lots. The neighborhood's architecture has real character — there are actual prewar details, original hardwood floors, and the kind of craftsmanship that doesn't show up in new construction. Forest Hills Tennis Stadium (home of the Western Open) is right next door, and Maple Grove Cemetery, one of Queens' quieter green spaces, borders the eastern edge of the neighborhood. Shopping and dining options are concentrated along Lefferts Boulevard, with a walkable, low-key main street feel.

Schools serving 11415 include PS 99, the elementary school that draws strong parent community involvement, as well as access to several specialized programs and gifted tracks available through the broader Queens district system. The neighborhood's diversity — it draws from across the world, with significant Jewish, South Asian, and Eastern European communities — makes it one of the more genuinely cosmopolitan zip codes in this part of Queens.

Frequently Asked Questions — Kew Gardens 11415

What is the difference between Kew Gardens and Forest Hills?

Kew Gardens (11415) and Forest Hills (11375) are adjacent and often compared, but they differ meaningfully. Forest Hills skews slightly higher in price, has a larger co-op concentration, and has a slightly more established retail corridor. Kew Gardens tends to offer better value per square foot, quieter residential blocks, and access to both the E/F at Union Turnpike and the Jamaica Ave commercial corridor. Many buyers who start in Forest Hills end up finding better value in Kew Gardens.

Are co-ops in Kew Gardens difficult to get approved for?

Co-op board requirements vary significantly by building in 11415. Some buildings are known for straightforward approval processes; others have strict income-to-price ratios, asset requirements, or limitations on subletting that can disqualify otherwise qualified buyers. Working with an agent who knows which buildings to target for your financial profile is essential. Nitin can help you assess co-op financials before you invest time in an application.

How good are the schools in Kew Gardens?

PS 99 and the surrounding elementary school options are well-regarded, with active parent communities and solid test scores relative to the borough average. For middle and high school, students access the broader Queens district system, which includes some competitive specialized programs. Many Kew Gardens families also use private and parochial school options, which are accessible due to the neighborhood's location.

Is Kew Gardens walkable?

Kew Gardens earns a Walk Score of roughly 85 — very walkable by NYC standards. Lefferts Boulevard has groceries, cafes, pharmacies, and restaurants within a short walk of most homes. The E/F train is walkable from most blocks in the zip, and the Q10, Q46, and other buses serve the area well for destinations not on the subway line.
