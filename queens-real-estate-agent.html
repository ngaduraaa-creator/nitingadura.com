What Makes Queens Real Estate Unique

Queens isn't like any other borough. Here's what buyers and sellers need to understand about this market.

Diverse Housing Stock Unlike Any Borough

Queens has co-ops, condos, semi-detached homes, attached row houses, two-family and three-family buildings — often on the same block. Understanding which type fits your needs and budget requires a local expert.

New York Is an Attorney State

Unlike most states, New York requires both buyers and sellers to hire real estate attorneys for the contract and closing process. Your agent and attorney work as a team. I can refer you to experienced Queens-area attorneys.

Co-op Board Approvals Are Real

Many Queens buildings — especially in Forest Hills, Rego Park, and Kew Gardens — are co-ops with strict board approval requirements. Financial documents, personal references, and interviews are standard. I help buyers prepare complete packages.

The Most Linguistically Diverse Place on Earth

Queens has over 160 languages spoken. Hindi and Punjabi are among the most common in South Ozone Park, Richmond Hill, and Jamaica. Having an agent who speaks your language is not a luxury — it's an advantage.

Frequently Asked Questions — Queens Real Estate

Common questions from buyers and sellers in Queens, answered by Nitin Gadura.

What is the average home price in Queens?
The average home price in Queens, NY is approximately $750,000 as of 2025–2026, though prices vary significantly by neighborhood. Astoria and Forest Hills tend to run higher, while neighborhoods like Southeast Queens (Springfield Gardens, Laurelton) offer more affordable options in the $450,000–$600,000 range. Two-family and multi-family homes in Ozone Park, Richmond Hill, and Jamaica typically range from $600,000 to $900,000.
How long does it take to sell a house in Queens?
In Queens, the average days on market (DOM) for a home ranges from 30 to 60 days in normal market conditions. Well-priced properties in desirable neighborhoods can go into contract within 1–2 weeks. The full closing process in New York typically takes 60–90 days after an accepted offer due to attorney review and mortgage contingency periods.
Do I need a real estate attorney in Queens, NY?
Yes. New York is an attorney state, meaning both buyers and sellers are required to have their own real estate attorney handle the contract review and closing process. Your real estate agent handles the negotiation and transaction coordination, while your attorney reviews contracts, handles title search, and represents you at closing. Nitin Gadura can refer you to experienced real estate attorneys familiar with Queens transactions.
