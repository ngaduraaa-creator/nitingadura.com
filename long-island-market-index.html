Long Island Neighborhoods Market Index — 2026

Data last compiled April 2026 · directional figures from recent OneKey® MLS activity for Nassau and Suffolk Counties · pull a transaction-specific CMA before pricing.

Long Island covers two counties, more than 130 residential ZIPs, and hundreds of school districts. This index groups the primary Gadura service-area submarkets by price tier, trade-off (commute vs. taxes vs. schools), and buyer profile.

Long Island property taxes are among the highest in the US. Nassau typically $12,000–$30,000+/year; Suffolk typically $9,000–$22,000+/year. The annual tax bill often moves the affordability math more than price does. If you think your assessment is off, read our Nassau & Suffolk grievance guide.

How to Pick a Long Island Town

  • Lowest all-in cost: Hempstead, Freeport, Baldwin, Uniondale
  • Best commute to Manhattan: Garden City, Rockville Centre (LIRR express stops)
  • Best schools per dollar: Syosset, Jericho, Bethpage, Merrick, Rockville Centre
  • Water/beach lifestyle: Long Beach, Freeport, Oceanside, Port Washington
  • South Asian / Indo-Caribbean community depth: Elmont, Hicksville, Floral Park, Franklin Square
  • Entry-level first-time buyer: Valley Stream, Elmont, Hempstead, Uniondale

Critical Commuter Math

The LIRR to Penn Station is the primary commute. Approximate times from major stations:

  • 15–20 min: Forest Hills, Kew Gardens, Jamaica (express stops)
  • 25–30 min: Floral Park, New Hyde Park, Rockville Centre, Garden City, Valley Stream
  • 30–40 min: Hicksville, Bethpage, Massapequa, Long Beach, Freeport, Hempstead
  • 40–55 min: Jericho, Syosset, Huntington, Babylon
  • 55+ min: Most of central and eastern Suffolk
