Brooklyn vs. Queens — What Buyers Should Know

Many of my clients ask whether to buy in Brooklyn or Queens. Here's an honest comparison to help you decide.

Brooklyn

  • Generally higher prices per square foot than Queens
  • More condos and co-ops relative to single-family homes
  • Fewer two-family investment properties in most neighborhoods
  • Premium Brooklyn neighborhoods ($1.5M+) are world-class
  • Strong appreciation in north Brooklyn (Bed-Stuy, Crown Heights, Bushwick)
  • Different transit lines (A/C, 2/3, 4/5, B/Q, F, N/R)
  • Strong Caribbean and West Indian community presence
  • More co-op buildings with board approval requirements

Queens

  • Generally more affordable on a per-square-foot basis
  • More semi-detached and two-family homes
  • More outdoor space per property on average
  • Large South Asian, Punjabi, Guyanese, and Caribbean communities
  • Closer to JFK airport and Southeast Queens towns
  • Direct Nassau County border for buyers eyeing Long Island
  • Strong multi-family investment opportunities
  • Nitin's home borough — deepest local knowledge here

The right answer depends on your budget, family size, community ties, and investment goals. Call Nitin at (917) 705-0132 for an honest, no-pressure conversation about which borough fits your situation.

Frequently Asked Questions — Brooklyn Real Estate

Common questions from Brooklyn buyers and sellers, answered directly by Nitin Gadura.

What is the average home price in Brooklyn?
The average home price in Brooklyn, NY is approximately $900,000 as of 2025–2026, though there is wide variation by neighborhood. Premium brownstone areas like Park Slope, Cobble Hill, and Carroll Gardens average $1.5M–$2M+. More affordable options exist in East New York, Canarsie, Brownsville, and Flatbush, where single-family and two-family homes range from $600,000 to $850,000.
Is Brooklyn or Queens better to buy in?
It depends on your priorities. Brooklyn is generally more expensive on a per-square-foot basis, with more condos and co-ops and fewer multi-family properties. Queens offers more two-family and semi-detached homes, often better value for space, and strong transit access. If you want more outdoor space and value, Queens often wins. If Brooklyn's specific culture, schools, or communities are your priority, the premium may be worth it. Call Nitin at (917) 705-0132 for a personalized comparison based on your budget and needs.
What Brooklyn neighborhoods are most affordable?
As of 2025–2026, the more affordable Brooklyn neighborhoods for buyers include East New York (ZIP 11207, 11208), Canarsie (ZIP 11236), Brownsville (ZIP 11212), Flatlands, and parts of Flatbush and Gravesend. These neighborhoods have seen appreciation in recent years but still offer entry-level pricing for single-family and two-family homes relative to north Brooklyn.
