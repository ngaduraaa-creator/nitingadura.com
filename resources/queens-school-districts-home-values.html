Queens School Districts & Home Values

How school zones shape property prices in Queens — and what every buyer and seller needs to know before making a decision.

Why School Zones Matter for Home Values

In Queens, as in most of New York City, school zone assignments are tied to your home's address — not a choice parents make after moving in. This means that two nearly identical homes on different sides of a school boundary can sell for meaningfully different prices. For buyers with children, the assigned school can be a non-negotiable factor that drives demand — and therefore pricing — in specific blocks and neighborhoods.

Research consistently shows that homes zoned for higher-performing schools sell faster and at premium prices. In Queens markets, this premium can range from a modest 5–8% above comparable homes in adjacent zones all the way to 15–20% in neighborhoods where access to a top-rated elementary or high school is highly contested.

The School Premium: What the Numbers Look Like

10–15%
Typical home value premium for properties zoned to a top-rated Queens elementary school compared to similar homes in adjacent zones.
15–20%
Premium observed in highly competitive zones — particularly Forest Hills and Bayside — where demand from families consistently outpaces supply.
3–6 days
Homes in top-rated school zones in Queens typically spend fewer days on market, with more competitive offer situations.

Note: Premiums vary by neighborhood, market conditions, and individual property factors. These figures are illustrative of observed trends and not guaranteed outcomes.

Notable Queens Schools That Influence Home Values

PS 196 — The Grand Central

Forest Hills • District 28 • Grades K–6

One of the most sought-after elementary zones in Queens. Forest Hills homes zoned for PS 196 consistently command premiums and see strong buyer competition.

PS 90 — Horace Mann

Ozone Park / Forest Hills Area • District 27

Well-regarded elementary serving parts of the Ozone Park and Richmond Hill corridor. Proximity to this school zone is a selling point in the district.

PS 178 — Holliswood School

Holliswood / Jamaica Estates • District 26

District 26 consistently ranks among the best in NYC. Homes in this zone — particularly in Jamaica Estates and Hollis Hills — carry a strong school-zone premium.

IS 93 — Ridgewood

Ridgewood / Ozone Park Area • Grades 6–8

A middle school with solid test scores serving parts of western Queens. Families in this zone benefit from a strong pipeline into well-regarded high schools.

PS 50 — Talfourd Lawn

Jamaica • District 29

Serves the Jamaica area; buyers seeking value with access to a solid elementary often target homes in this zone as an entry point to the neighborhood.

Francis Lewis High School

Fresh Meadows • District 26 High School

One of Queens' highest-ranked large public high schools. Homes in the Francis Lewis zone (District 26, eastern Queens) are among the most sought-after for families with teenagers.

Townsend Harris High School

Flushing • NYC Specialized / Screened

Ranked among the top public high schools in the US, Townsend Harris is screened (not strictly zone-based) but its location in Flushing adds prestige to the surrounding area.

Bayside High School

Bayside • District 26

A large, well-regarded comprehensive high school in northeastern Queens. The Bayside and Douglaston markets are partly driven by this school zone for high schoolers.

How to Find Your School Zone

School zone boundaries in NYC are set by the NYC Department of Education and can change — sometimes significantly — in a single school year. Always verify directly with the NYCDOE before making or accepting any offer based on a specific school assignment.

  1. Visit myschools.nyc and use the "Find a School" tool with your exact address to see your assigned zoned school at each level (elementary, middle, high school).
  2. Call or email the specific school district office directly — for example, District 27 serves Ozone Park, Richmond Hill, and Howard Beach; District 26 serves Bayside, Hollis Hills, and Jamaica Estates.
  3. Ask your real estate attorney to confirm the school zone assignment in writing if it is a material factor in your purchase decision. This can be included as a contract contingency in some cases.
  4. Note that screened and specialized schools (like Townsend Harris, Queens High School for the Sciences) are not zone-based — they admit students city-wide based on academic criteria.

Important Disclaimer: School zone boundaries can and do change. The schools referenced on this page are illustrative of neighborhoods and trends — they are not a guarantee of any buyer's actual school assignment. Always verify directly with the NYC Department of Education (nyc.gov/doe) using the current address before relying on any school zone information in a real estate decision.

For Sellers: Leveraging Your School Zone

If your home sits in a desirable school zone, your marketing strategy should highlight it prominently — but carefully, in compliance with Fair Housing laws. You can state the assigned school and direct buyers to verify zoning themselves; you cannot make promises about school quality or enrollment guarantees.

Consider the following in your pricing and marketing strategy:

  • Identify which buyer demographic in your price range is most likely to have school-age children — and time your listing accordingly (spring is peak family-buying season).
  • In your listing description, note the assigned elementary, middle, and high school for the address, and include the school district number.
  • If your home is near — but not in — a top school zone, be accurate. Buyers will verify, and a misrepresentation on school zone is a serious liability.
  • Ask me to run a Comparative Market Analysis (CMA) that specifically controls for school zone — this gives you the clearest picture of how much your zone is actually worth.
