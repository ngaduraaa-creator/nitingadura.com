First-Time Homebuyer Programs
in Queens, NY — 2024 Guide

Down payment assistance, low-interest mortgages, and grant programs that can help Queens residents buy their first home — including up to $100,000 in forgivable assistance.

Buying your first home in Queens is achievable — but it requires knowing what programs exist and how to stack them together. The programs below are available to income-qualified buyers purchasing in Queens. Program terms, income limits, and availability change regularly; always verify current terms with the administering agency or a participating lender. After reading this guide, call me — I'll connect you with the right lender contacts and walk you through the process.

SONYMA — Achieving the Dream

NY State Program

The State of New York Mortgage Agency's (SONYMA) flagship first-time buyer loan offers below-market fixed interest rates. "Achieving the Dream" is specifically designed for low-to-moderate income buyers and offers some of the lowest rates in the state — typically 1–2% below conventional mortgage rates.

As low as 3%
620+
~$98K–$155K depending on household size
1–4 family, condos, co-ops

Eligible properties include 1–4 family homes, condos, and co-ops. The purchase price limit for Queens is set by SONYMA annually and is typically higher than most downstate markets. Buyers must complete an approved homebuyer education course to qualify.

How to apply: Apply through a SONYMA-participating lender. A list is available at hcr.ny.gov. I can refer you to local lenders familiar with this program.

SONYMA — Low Interest Rate Program

NY State Program

The SONYMA Low Interest Rate Program serves buyers with slightly higher incomes than the Achieving the Dream threshold, while still offering below-market 30-year fixed rates. This program also allows down payment assistance to be layered on top — making it a strong foundation for a Queens purchase.

As low as 3%
30-yr fixed, below market
~$124K–$195K depending on household size
NYC HomeFirst, SONYMA DPA

Like all SONYMA programs, buyers must not have owned a primary residence in the past 3 years (with exceptions for federally designated target areas in Queens). Required homebuyer education is a prerequisite.

NYC HomeFirst Down Payment Assistance

Up to $100,000

The NYC HomeFirst program provides up to $100,000 in down payment and closing cost assistance for first-time homebuyers purchasing in the five boroughs — including Queens. The assistance comes as a forgivable loan: if you remain in the home for the required period (typically 10–15 years depending on loan amount), the entire amount is forgiven with no repayment required.

Up to $100,000
Forgivable loan (0% interest)
~$74,480 AMI (2024)
~$106,400 AMI (2024)

To qualify, buyers must: be a first-time homebuyer (no ownership in prior 3 years), have income at or below 80% of Area Median Income (AMI), contribute at least 3% of their own funds, complete an approved 8-hour homebuyer education course, and obtain a primary mortgage from a participating lender.

The program is administered by the NYC Department of Housing Preservation and Development (HPD). Demand is high and funding is periodically exhausted — contact HPD or a participating lender early to confirm current availability.

Stacking Strategy: NYC HomeFirst can often be combined with a SONYMA Low Interest Rate mortgage, giving buyers both a below-market interest rate AND up to $100K toward their down payment. For many Queens buyers, this combination is the most powerful entry-point tool available.

FHA Loans — Federal Housing Administration

Federal Program

FHA loans are insured by the federal government and available through most banks and mortgage lenders. They are one of the most popular paths to homeownership for Queens first-time buyers because of their low down payment requirement and flexible credit standards.

3.5% (with 580+ score)
580 (3.5% down) / 500 (10% down)
~$1,149,825 (2024 high-cost area limit)
Yes — upfront + annual

FHA loans require Mortgage Insurance Premium (MIP) — both an upfront fee (1.75% of the loan) and an annual fee. The annual MIP typically lasts the life of the loan unless you refinance into a conventional loan later. Despite this cost, FHA remains the most accessible financing for buyers with lower credit scores or minimal savings.

FHA loans in Queens can be used for 1–4 family homes (buyer must occupy one unit) and approved condos. Co-ops do not qualify for FHA financing.

HCR — Homes and Community Renewal Grants

NY State

New York State's Homes and Community Renewal (HCR) agency administers several programs that can provide additional assistance for first-time buyers in eligible areas. These include the Neighborhood Stabilization Program and various community development grants administered through local nonprofit housing organizations in Queens.

Grants & low-interest loans
HCR + local nonprofits
Income-based, varies by program

HCR programs often operate through local housing counseling agencies in Queens such as Neighborhood Housing Services of Jamaica (NHS Jamaica) and La Guardia Community Development Corporation. These organizations can also provide free homebuyer counseling that fulfills the education requirement for SONYMA and HomeFirst programs.

USDA Loans — Not Available in Queens

Not Applicable

USDA Rural Development loans offer 0% down payment financing — but only in areas that USDA designates as "rural." Queens, as part of New York City, does not qualify. If you are considering properties in eastern Long Island or upstate New York, USDA may be an option to explore, but it is not a tool for Queens purchases.

How to Get Started — Step by Step

1

Complete a Homebuyer Education Course

Most Queens programs (HomeFirst, SONYMA) require an 8-hour HUD-approved homebuyer education course. Take this early — it's free or low-cost and satisfies requirements for multiple programs simultaneously. Search for approved courses at hud.gov or through NHS Jamaica.

2

Get Pre-Approved with a Program-Participating Lender

Not all lenders participate in SONYMA or HomeFirst. Contact a SONYMA-approved lender who is also an HPD-participating lender — they can assess your eligibility for multiple programs at once and stack the benefits. I can provide referrals to lenders I work with regularly in Queens.

3

Understand Your Full Buying Power

With SONYMA + HomeFirst potentially combined, a qualifying buyer could receive a below-market rate mortgage AND up to $100K in forgivable assistance. Know your total purchasing power before you start shopping — it may be significantly higher than you expect.

4

Contact Nitin — Search Starts Here

Once you are pre-approved and know your target price range, let's start searching. I specialize in Queens and know which neighborhoods, building types, and sellers are the best fit for first-time buyers using assistance programs. Some sellers are specifically motivated to work with program buyers.

Program terms change frequently. Income limits, purchase price caps, and available funding for HomeFirst and SONYMA are updated regularly. The figures on this page are based on 2024 program parameters. Always verify current terms directly with the administering agency or a participating lender before making financial decisions.
