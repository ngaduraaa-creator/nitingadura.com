Agency Disclosure — NY Real Property Law §443

New York State requires that this disclosure be provided before entering into a buyer or seller representation agreement, and prior to the first showing of any property. Please read carefully.

Legal Requirement: New York State law (NY Real Property Law §443) requires that before entering into a buyer or seller representation agreement, and prior to the first showing of a property to a prospective buyer, Gadura Real Estate LLC must provide this disclosure. By receiving and acknowledging this disclosure, you understand the nature of the agency relationship being offered. Please read it carefully and ask questions before proceeding.

Disclosure Regarding Real Estate Agency Relationships

As required by New York Real Property Law §443  |  Gadura Real Estate LLC

This disclosure is provided to help you understand the different types of agency relationships that exist in New York real estate transactions. New York law requires real estate licensees acting as agents of buyers or sellers of property to advise potential buyers or sellers with whom they work of the nature of their agency relationship and the rights and obligations it creates.

Type A

Seller's Agent (Listing Agent)

A seller's agent (also known as a listing agent) represents the seller exclusively. The seller's agent owes the seller the following fiduciary duties:

Seller's Agent

Working Solely for the Seller

A seller's agent is retained by and works exclusively on behalf of the seller. All duties run to the seller.

  • Undivided loyalty to the seller — must put the seller's interests above all others
  • Must disclose all material information to the seller that may affect the transaction
  • Must negotiate the best possible price and terms for the seller
  • Must maintain confidentiality of the seller's information
  • May NOT disclose the seller's motivation for selling without the seller's permission
  • May NOT disclose that the seller will accept less than the asking price without the seller's permission
  • May NOT disclose any information the seller has asked to keep confidential
  • Must present all offers to the seller in a timely manner

Note: A buyer dealing with a seller's agent is a customer, not a client, and is NOT represented by that agent.

Type B

Buyer's Agent

A buyer's agent represents the buyer exclusively. The buyer's agent owes the buyer the following fiduciary duties:

Buyer's Agent

Working Solely for the Buyer

A buyer's agent is retained by and works exclusively on behalf of the buyer. All duties run to the buyer.

  • Undivided loyalty to the buyer — must put the buyer's interests above all others
  • Must disclose all material information to the buyer that may affect the transaction
  • Must negotiate the best possible price and terms for the buyer
  • Must maintain confidentiality of the buyer's information
  • May NOT disclose the buyer's motivation for buying without the buyer's permission
  • May NOT disclose that the buyer will pay more than offered without the buyer's permission
  • May NOT disclose the buyer's financial capacity beyond what is necessary
  • Must conduct due diligence on properties on behalf of the buyer

Note: A seller dealing with a buyer's agent is a customer, not a client, and is NOT represented by that agent.

Type C

Broker's Agent

Broker's Agent

Working for a Listing or Buyer's Agent's Firm

A broker's agent does not have a direct relationship with either the buyer or seller but works on behalf of another licensee's client. For example, a cooperating broker who assists a buyer's agent still serves the buyer — the broker's agent's duties run through the agent to the agent's client.

  • Works on behalf of (and owes duties to) the other agent's client
  • Can assist a buyer's agent in finding or showing properties, while working for the buyer
  • Can assist a seller's agent in marketing or showing a listing, while working for the seller
  • Does not independently represent either the buyer or seller
Type D

Dual Agent

Dual Agent

Representing Both Buyer and Seller — With Limitations

A dual agent represents both the buyer and the seller in the same transaction. Dual agency is legal in New York but creates inherent limitations on the duties that can be provided to each party. Both parties must give their informed written consent before a dual agency relationship can exist.

  • A dual agent cannot provide undivided loyalty to either party
  • A dual agent cannot disclose to the buyer the minimum price the seller will accept
  • A dual agent cannot disclose to the seller the maximum price the buyer will pay
  • A dual agent cannot disclose motivation or confidential information of either party to the other
  • Both parties must give written consent acknowledging they understand the limitations of dual agency
  • The dual agent must remain neutral and cannot advocate for either side in negotiations

Important: If dual agency arises, you will be notified and asked to provide written consent at that time. You have the right to refuse dual agency representation.

Type E

Disclosed Dual Agent

Disclosed Dual Agent

Both Parties Agree in Writing to Dual Representation

A disclosed dual agent is created when both the buyer and the seller agree in writing, with full knowledge, that the agent represents both sides of the transaction simultaneously. The agent discloses all relationships and both parties accept them.

  • Requires affirmative written consent from both buyer and seller
  • Agent must remain neutral and cannot advocate for either party
  • Both parties understand and accept the limitations on confidentiality and advocacy
  • Agent retains general duties of care, honesty, and disclosure of material facts
Type F

Non-Agent (Transaction Broker)

Non-Agent

Facilitating the Transaction Without Representing Either Party

A non-agent (also called a transaction broker) does not represent either the buyer or the seller and has no fiduciary duties to either party. The non-agent assists in facilitating a real estate transaction but cannot advocate for either side.

  • No fiduciary duties owed to either buyer or seller
  • Can facilitate paperwork, coordinate logistics, and assist with administrative tasks
  • Cannot negotiate on behalf of either party
  • Cannot advise either party on price or strategy
  • Neither party is represented — both are customers
Firm Policy

Gadura Real Estate LLC Agency Policy

Our Standard Practice

Gadura Real Estate LLC represents both buyers and sellers in separate, exclusive agency relationships. When representing a seller, Nitin Gadura acts as the seller's agent. When representing a buyer, Nitin Gadura acts as the buyer's agent.

Before proceeding with any representation, clients will be asked to sign the appropriate representation agreement — either an Exclusive Right to Sell Listing Agreement (for sellers) or a Buyer Representation Agreement (for buyers). The type of agency relationship, the term, and the compensation arrangement will be fully specified in writing before any services begin.

If a situation arises in which Gadura Real Estate LLC would represent both a buyer and a seller in the same transaction (dual agency), both parties will be immediately notified and asked for written consent. You have the right to decline dual agency at any time.

2024 Update

Compensation Disclosure — NAR Settlement (August 17, 2024)

Important Change Effective August 17, 2024

Pursuant to the NAR Settlement Agreement (Burnett et al. v. National Association of Realtors), the following changes are now in effect for all real estate transactions:

1. Buyer Representation Agreement Required: Buyers must enter into a written Buyer Representation Agreement before touring any property. This agreement will specify the term, geographic area, property type, and compensation arrangement for buyer representation services.

2. Compensation Not in MLS: Buyer broker compensation is no longer included as a field in MLS listings. Sellers are not obligated to offer buyer agent compensation through the MLS.

3. Compensation is Negotiable: Compensation for buyer representation is fully negotiable and will be disclosed in the written Buyer Representation Agreement. Buyers and sellers may negotiate compensation arrangements directly.

4. Seller Concessions: A seller may still offer concessions (including amounts that a buyer may use for buyer agent compensation) as part of their listing, disclosed outside of MLS.
