Equal Housing Opportunity

Fair Housing Policy

Gadura Real Estate LLC and Nitin Gadura are fully committed to federal, New York State, and New York City fair housing laws. Everyone deserves equal access to housing — no exceptions.

Section 1

Equal Housing Opportunity Statement

Nitin K. Gadura, Licensed NYS Real Estate Salesperson, and Gadura Real Estate LLC are committed to the letter and spirit of U.S. policy for the achievement of equal housing opportunity throughout the nation. We encourage and support an affirmative advertising and marketing program in which there are no barriers to obtaining housing because of race, color, religion, sex, handicap, familial status, national origin, or any other class protected by law.

This commitment extends to every aspect of our business: how we market properties, who we show homes to, how we present offers, and how we treat every client and consumer regardless of background.

Section 2

Federal Fair Housing Act (42 U.S.C. §3601 et seq.)

The Federal Fair Housing Act, enacted in 1968 and amended in 1988, prohibits discrimination in the sale, rental, and financing of housing based on the following protected classes:

Federally Protected Classes

Race Color National Origin Religion Sex Familial Status Disability (Physical or Mental)

Sex includes gender identity, gender expression, and sexual harassment. Familial Status protects families with children under the age of 18 and pregnant women. Disability covers both physical and mental impairments that substantially limit a major life activity.

Discrimination can occur in many forms, including:

  • Steering — directing buyers or renters toward or away from neighborhoods based on their race, color, religion, or national origin
  • Blockbusting — inducing homeowners to sell by suggesting that demographic changes will reduce property values
  • Redlining — refusing to provide real estate services in certain neighborhoods based on their racial or ethnic composition
  • Discriminatory advertising — using words, photos, or descriptions that indicate a preference or limitation based on a protected class
  • Refusing to show a property or provide information about a listing
  • Offering different terms, conditions, or services to different buyers or renters based on protected class
  • Misrepresenting the availability of housing ("that unit is already rented" — when it is not)
Section 3

New York State Human Rights Law (NY Exec Law §296)

New York State law extends fair housing protections significantly beyond the federal minimum. In addition to all federal classes, the following are protected under New York State law:

NY State Additional Protected Classes

Age Creed Sexual Orientation Gender Identity or Expression Military Status Marital Status Source of Income Domestic Violence Victim Status

The Source of Income protection is particularly important: it is illegal under New York State law to refuse to work with, show property to, or otherwise discriminate against a person because of how their housing is paid for — including Housing Choice Vouchers (Section 8), Social Security Income, disability benefits, and other lawful income sources.

The NY State Division of Human Rights enforces these protections and can investigate and adjudicate complaints.

Section 4

New York City Human Rights Law (NYC Admin Code §8-107)

The New York City Human Rights Law is one of the broadest anti-discrimination laws in the country. It applies to real estate transactions in all five boroughs — Queens, Brooklyn, Manhattan, the Bronx, and Staten Island — and protects the following additional classes beyond state and federal law:

NYC Additional Protected Classes

Lawful Source of Income (incl. Section 8, public assistance, disability benefits) Citizenship and Immigration Status Partnership Status Unemployment Status Victim of Domestic Violence, Stalking, or Sex Offenses Consumer Credit History Arrest Record or Criminal Conviction History Caregiver Status

Under NYC law, a housing provider cannot ask about a prospective buyer's or renter's credit score for the purpose of denying housing, nor use an arrest record or sealed conviction in a discriminatory way. The NYC Commission on Human Rights actively enforces these provisions.

Section 5

Source of Income / Section 8 Policy

Gadura Real Estate LLC and Nitin Gadura do not discriminate based on source of income. We work with buyers and renters who use Housing Choice Vouchers (Section 8), Social Security Income (SSI), disability benefits, public assistance, and all other lawful sources of income. Refusing to work with a Section 8 voucher holder is illegal under both New York State law (NY Exec Law §296) and New York City law (NYC Admin Code §8-107).

This policy applies universally. Whether a client is paying cash, using conventional financing, using an FHA loan, or using a Housing Choice Voucher, they are entitled to the same quality of service, the same properties, and the same professional representation.

If you are a Housing Choice Voucher holder and have experienced discrimination in the housing search process, you are encouraged to file a complaint with the NYC Commission on Human Rights or the NY State Division of Human Rights (contact information in Section 7 below).

Section 6

What Constitutes Illegal Housing Discrimination

The following conduct is illegal under federal, state, or city fair housing law and will never be practiced or condoned by Gadura Real Estate LLC or Nitin Gadura:

  • Refusing to sell, rent, or show housing to any person based on their membership in a protected class
  • Steering buyers or renters toward or away from certain neighborhoods based on race, ethnicity, religion, national origin, or any other protected characteristic
  • Making discriminatory statements in advertising, listings, marketing materials, or in any conversation with a consumer
  • Providing different terms, conditions, services, or pricing to different buyers or renters based on protected class
  • Blockbusting — inducing panic-selling by suggesting that the demographic character of a neighborhood is changing
  • Redlining — denying real estate services in particular neighborhoods based on the racial or demographic composition of those neighborhoods
  • Misrepresenting the availability of a property ("that unit is taken") when it is not, in order to avoid showing to a protected class member
  • Retaliating against any person who asserts their rights under fair housing laws, files a complaint, or cooperates with a fair housing investigation
  • Refusing to accept or process an offer based on the protected characteristics of the buyer
  • Refusing to work with a client because of their source of income, including Section 8, public assistance, or disability benefits
Section 7

How to File a Fair Housing Complaint

If you believe you have experienced housing discrimination, you have the right to file a complaint. You do not need an attorney. Complaints can be filed with any of the following agencies:

U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD)

1-800-669-9777 (toll-free)
hud.gov/fairhousing
Online complaint portal available. Deadline: 1 year from discriminatory act.

NY State Division of Human Rights

(888) 392-3644
dhr.ny.gov
Offices in NYC and across New York State. Deadline: 1 year.

NYC Commission on Human Rights

(212) 416-0197
nyc.gov/cchr
For NYC-specific violations. Deadline: 3 years (NYC law).

NY Department of State (License Complaints)

(518) 474-4429
dos.ny.gov
To file a complaint against a licensed real estate salesperson or broker.

Filing a fair housing complaint is free of charge. You are protected from retaliation for filing a complaint.

Section 8

Our Commitment

Fair housing is not just a legal obligation — it is a core value. Nitin Gadura and Gadura Real Estate LLC believe that every person deserves equal access to safe, stable housing and to professional real estate representation, regardless of their background.

We participate in ongoing fair housing training, maintain this written policy, and apply our procedures uniformly to all clients and consumers. If you have experienced any conduct from our firm that you believe violated these principles, we want to know immediately.
