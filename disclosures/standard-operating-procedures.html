Standard Operating Procedures — NY Real Property Law §442-H

New York State requires all licensed real estate brokers and salespersons to maintain written Standard Operating Procedures describing how they work with prospective buyers and sellers.

Legal Requirement: New York State law (NY Real Property Law §442-H) requires all real estate brokers and salespersons to maintain and provide written Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) describing how our firm works with prospective buyers and sellers. These procedures must be provided upon request and must be applied uniformly and consistently to all consumers regardless of protected class.

Part I: Procedures for Prospective Buyers

1

Identification Requirements

Prospective buyers are not required to provide government-issued identification prior to touring a property solely because they are working with Gadura Real Estate LLC.

Identification may be requested in the following limited circumstances:

  • The seller or listing agent requires ID verification prior to access as a condition of showing their property (applied uniformly to all buyers)
  • Access to a gated community or co-op building requires visitor identification per building security policy
Note: Any identification requirement must be applied uniformly to all buyers regardless of protected class. Gadura Real Estate LLC will never impose identification requirements based on race, national origin, immigration status, or any other protected characteristic.
2

Pre-Approval / Proof of Funds

For certain listings — particularly those with specific seller requirements — buyers may be asked to provide proof of mortgage pre-approval or proof of funds prior to scheduling a showing. This is a seller requirement, not a firm policy, and is disclosed at the time of scheduling.

This requirement, when applicable, is applied uniformly to all prospective buyers for that specific listing and is not applied based on protected class.
3

Buyer Representation Agreement (NAR Settlement — Effective August 17, 2024)

Effective August 17, 2024, in compliance with the NAR Settlement Agreement, Gadura Real Estate LLC requires all prospective buyers to sign a written Buyer Representation Agreement before touring any property.

The Buyer Representation Agreement will specify:

  • The term of the agreement (start and end date)
  • The geographic area covered
  • The type of property covered
  • The compensation arrangement for buyer representation services (compensation is negotiable)
Compensation is fully negotiable. Buyer agent compensation is no longer offered through the MLS. The agreement will specify the amount and structure of any buyer agent compensation, which may be paid by the buyer, by the seller as a concession, or through another arrangement agreed upon in writing.
4

Showing Requirements and Scheduling

Properties are shown by appointment, coordinated with the listing agent or seller. Scheduling is subject to seller availability and reasonable advance notice (typically 24 hours, though urgent requests can often be accommodated).

Virtual tours and video walkthroughs may be available for out-of-state buyers or buyers with scheduling constraints. This option is discussed on a property-by-property basis.

5

Making an Offer

All offers must be submitted in writing. Verbal offers are not binding and will be confirmed in writing before submission.

Offers are presented to the seller or seller's representative promptly upon receipt. All offers — including those the seller may be unlikely to accept — are presented to the seller unless the seller has provided specific written instructions otherwise.

Counteroffers and responses from the seller are communicated to the buyer promptly and in writing.

6

Due Diligence Assistance

Gadura Real Estate LLC will assist buyers in coordinating inspections, reviewing title reports, and understanding transaction timelines. Buyers are encouraged to independently select and retain their own attorney, inspector, and other advisors.

Part II: Procedures for Prospective Sellers

1

Listing Agreement

Sellers must sign a written Exclusive Right to Sell Listing Agreement before any marketing begins. This agreement will specify:

  • The listing price
  • The listing term (start and end date)
  • The commission/compensation arrangement
  • Marketing authorizations (MLS, photographs, lockbox, virtual tours, etc.)
  • Any seller-imposed showing conditions or restrictions
2

Property Condition Disclosure Statement (NY RPL §462)

Sellers of residential 1-4 unit property are required by New York law to complete and deliver a Property Condition Disclosure Statement (PCDS) to the buyer before the buyer signs the contract of sale.

Alternatively, sellers may elect to provide the buyer with a $500 credit at closing in lieu of completing the PCDS. Sellers should discuss this option with their attorney.

Most real estate attorneys recommend completing the PCDS honestly and thoroughly. Completing the PCDS accurately does not eliminate the need for a buyer's home inspection.
3

Pricing Strategy & CMA

Gadura Real Estate LLC will provide a Comparative Market Analysis (CMA) at no charge to assist sellers in determining an appropriate list price. The CMA analyzes recent comparable sales, active competition, and market conditions.

The final list price decision rests solely with the seller.

4

Marketing Plan

Listings will be marketed through a combination of channels including:

  • OneKey MLS (covering Queens, Long Island, and the tri-state region)
  • Zillow, Realtor.com, Homes.com, and syndicated portals
  • Professional photography and, where appropriate, video tours
  • Digital advertising and social media marketing
  • Targeted outreach to buyer prospects in our network
  • Open houses (at seller's discretion)
5

Offer Presentation

All written offers received on a listing will be presented to the seller promptly upon receipt — including offers the seller may be unlikely to accept, unless the seller has provided specific written direction otherwise.

When multiple offers are received, all offers will be presented to the seller. The seller makes all decisions regarding which offer, if any, to accept, counter, or reject. Gadura Real Estate LLC will provide guidance and market context but will not make acceptance decisions on the seller's behalf.

6

Transaction Coordination

After an offer is accepted, Gadura Real Estate LLC will assist with coordinating: attorney introduction and contract preparation timeline, inspection scheduling, mortgage contingency timeline tracking, title and survey coordination, and closing logistics.

Sellers are strongly encouraged to engage a licensed New York real estate attorney. Gadura Real Estate LLC does not provide legal advice.
